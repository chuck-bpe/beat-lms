# BEAT Curriculum Review

## Current Shape

- Format: 12-week self-paced bootcamp
- Cadence: about 2 hours per week
- Structure per week: lesson, demo, assignment, quiz
- Tone: practical, calm, recruiting-first

## Likely Reasons It May Feel Off

- It reads like a strong generic AI-enablement bootcamp, but not yet like a deeply customized Blueprint Expansion recruiting operating system.
- It emphasizes skill categories more than your exact workflows, tools, expectations, and team standards.
- It assumes a neat weekly progression, but not necessarily the real order in which your team needs to adopt your existing systems.
- It includes good concepts on agents, but may not feel concrete enough around “what exact agents should our team actually use or build?”
- It probably underrepresents your proprietary workflows, your dashboard, and the actual performance gains you care about.

## Suggested Revision Directions

### Option 1: Tool Adoption First

- Reorganize the curriculum around the Lovable recruiting dashboard and your existing internal workflows first
- Move generic AI literacy later or compress it
- Make the first 4 weeks about using your system correctly and consistently

### Option 2: Recruiting Workflow First

- Organize by real recruiting stages:
  - intake
  - sourcing
  - outreach
  - screening
  - client/candidate communication
  - workflow automation
- Teach Claude/ChatGPT/agents only inside those stages

### Option 3: Performance System First

- Frame each module around measurable gains:
  - faster sourcing
  - better outreach response rates
  - cleaner candidate evaluation
  - more consistent CRM/dashboard usage
- Make each week answer:
  - what exact output should improve?
  - what tool should they use?
  - what standard are they held to?

## My Recommendation

The current curriculum is solid as a scaffold, but I would not treat it as the final curriculum.

I would rebuild it around your real recruiting operating model:

1. your dashboard and existing internal tools
2. your exact sourcing and outreach workflows
3. the team behaviors you want enforced
4. the specific agent/use-case examples you want adopted

## Best Next Step

Create a Version 2 curriculum outline before rewriting lessons.

The fastest useful sequence is:

1. decide the right organizing principle
2. rewrite the 12-week outline at the module level only
3. review that outline together
4. then rewrite the week-by-week content
